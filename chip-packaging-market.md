<p><p>Chip packaging refers to the process of enclosing semiconductor chips to protect them and facilitate integration into electronic devices. Traditional packaging includes methods such as dual in-line packages (DIP) and surface-mount technology (SMT), focusing on basic functionality and cost-effectiveness. In contrast, advanced packaging encompasses more sophisticated techniques like system-in-package (SiP), 3D packaging, and flip-chip technologies, enabling higher performance, compact designs, and improved thermal management to cater to the evolving demands of modern electronics.</p></p>
