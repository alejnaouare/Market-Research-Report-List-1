<p><p>The hemodialysis access catheters market is divided into short-term and long-term catheters. Short-term catheters are typically used for acute dialysis needs, providing immediate access to a patient's bloodstream, usually for fewer than three weeks. In contrast, long-term catheters are designed for patients requiring extended dialysis treatment, functioning effectively for several months or even years. These catheters facilitate blood removal and return during the dialysis process, ensuring essential kidney function support for patients with renal failure.</p></p>
