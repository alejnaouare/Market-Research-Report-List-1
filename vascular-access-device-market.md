<p><strong>The Vascular Access Device Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Central Vascular Access Devices</li><li>Peripheral Vascular Access Devices</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The vascular access device market is categorized into central and peripheral vascular access devices. Central vascular access devices (CVADs) are inserted into large veins, providing long-term access for delivering medications, fluids, and nutrition, often used in critical care. Peripheral vascular access devices (PVADs), on the other hand, are inserted into smaller veins and are typically used for short-term treatments, such as delivering medications or drawing blood. Both types cater to different patient needs and treatment durations.</p></p>
