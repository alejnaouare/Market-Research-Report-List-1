<p><p>The carbon fiber-resin composite market is primarily divided into two types: thermosetting and thermoplastic. Thermosetting composites, which cure permanently when heated, offer superior mechanical properties and thermal resistance, making them ideal for aerospace and automotive applications. In contrast, thermoplastic composites can be re-molded upon heating, providing greater flexibility in manufacturing and recyclability. This distinction influences their usage in various industries, with thermosetting products favored for their durability and thermoplastic materials appreciated for ease of processing and sustainability.</p></p>
