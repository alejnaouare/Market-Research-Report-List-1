<p><p>The iron chelating agent market comprises three primary types: Deferasirox, Deferoxamine, and Deferiprone. Deferasirox is an oral medication commonly used to treat chronic iron overload due to blood transfusions, offering a convenient once-daily dosing. Deferoxamine is an injectable agent, traditionally used in acute iron toxicity and chronic conditions, known for its efficacy but requiring multiple doses. Deferiprone is also an oral option, effective in removing excess iron but associated with certain side effects. Each has unique benefits and use cases in managing iron overload.</p></p>
