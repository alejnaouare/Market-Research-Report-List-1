<p><p>Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) is an advanced technology used to reduce nitrogen oxide emissions from the exhaust of internal combustion engines. It involves injecting a reductant, typically urea, into the engine's exhaust stream, where it reacts with nitrogen oxides in the presence of a catalyst to convert them into harmless nitrogen and water vapor. This technology is widely utilized in automotive, industrial, and marine applications to meet stringent emissions regulations and improve air quality.</p><p>The Selective Catalytic Reduction (SCR) Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 6.1% during the forecast period. The automotive sector is particularly significant, with ongoing developments in diesel and gasoline-powered engines enhancing SCR adoption. Additionally, innovations in catalyst materials and system designs are improving the efficiency and effectiveness of SCR systems.
